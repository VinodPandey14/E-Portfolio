Weekly Learning

Week-1 : Introduction

In our lab sessions, I learned how to effectively introduce myself in various professional and academic settings. The importance of confidence, clarity, and structuring an introduction was emphasized. We practiced crafting introductions that included our name, academic background, interests, and career aspirations. Additionally, I learned how to tailor my introduction based on the audience, whether in interviews, networking events, or formal presentations. The session also covered non-verbal cues like eye contact, tone, and body language to leave a lasting impression. This helped me refine my ability to introduce myself in both casual and professional settings with ease.

Week-2 : Resume

The session on resumes taught me the significance of creating a well-structured and impactful resume to highlight my skills and achievements. I learned about different resume formats, including chronological, functional, and hybrid, and when to use each one. Emphasis was placed on writing strong bullet points that showcase accomplishments using action verbs and quantifiable results. We also covered the importance of ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) and how to optimize resumes with keywords for better visibility. Additionally, I practiced customizing my resume for specific job roles by focusing on relevant skills and experiences. This session helped me build a professional and polished resume that effectively represents my qualifications.

Week-3 : Extempore

Through our extempore sessions, I developed the ability to think quickly and articulate my thoughts clearly under time constraints. I learned how to structure my response in a logical flow—starting with an introduction, presenting key points, and concluding effectively. The session emphasized the importance of staying calm, organizing thoughts quickly, and delivering ideas confidently. I practiced speaking on random topics, which improved my spontaneity and fluency. Additionally, I gained insights into handling difficult topics by breaking them down into smaller ideas and supporting them with examples. This experience enhanced my communication skills and prepared me for public speaking and interview scenarios.

Week-4 : Movie Review

Movie: The Shawshank Redemption

Review: The Shawshank Redemption is a powerful film about hope, perseverance, and redemption. It follows Andy Dufresne, a wrongfully convicted banker, who navigates prison life while secretly planning his escape. The movie teaches resilience, as Andy’s patience and optimism keep him going despite adversity. His deep friendship with Red highlights the value of trust and support in difficult times. The film also explores the effects of institutionalization and the struggle for freedom. Andy’s escape and exposure of corruption serve as poetic justice, reinforcing the theme of redemption.

Favourite Dialogue: "Hope is a good thing, maybe the best of things, and no good thing ever dies." This dialogue is inspiring as it emphasizes the power of hope in overcoming challenges. It reminds us that no matter how tough life gets, hope can guide us toward a better future.

Learning Takeaways: The film teaches the importance of resilience, the power of friendship, and the need to embrace change. It also highlights the flaws in the justice system and the impact of institutionalization on individuals.

Related Classroom Concepts: The movie connects with concepts like resilience and mindset, ethics and justice, and communication skills—topics that we have explored in self-development and ethics discussions.

Week-5 : Public Speaking

Today's Learning

Public speaking, once a fear-inducing challenge for me, turned into a transformative experience this week. I learned that the heart of good public speaking lies in genuine connection — with the topic and the audience. It’s not about perfection, but presence. The session emphasized crafting an engaging introduction, maintaining a natural flow, and using pauses to add weight to words. I practiced vocal modulation — changing tone, pace, and pitch — to avoid sounding monotonous. Through JAM and extempore activities, I discovered how quick thinking and calmness can work together. I also understood that strong body language, eye contact, and gestures are not just enhancements — they’re essential. This session gave me the confidence to face an audience and speak with purpose, not fear.

Week-6 : Essay Writing

Essay writing taught me how to give form and structure to my thoughts, turning raw ideas into impactful expressions. I learned how to write clear and concise introductions that set the stage, develop body paragraphs that offer depth and analysis, and end with a conclusion that leaves a lasting impression. The session also highlighted the importance of transitions between ideas, maintaining a consistent tone, and avoiding grammatical pitfalls. We practiced brainstorming, outlining, and refining drafts — which taught me that great writing is often the result of great editing. Most importantly, I discovered how to express opinions respectfully, support arguments with logic, and make writing reflect my personality. Essay writing is no longer a formality to me — it has become a tool to express who I am and what I believe in.

Week-7 : Group Discussion

The group discussion session taught me the delicate balance between assertiveness and empathy. I realized that being a good speaker is only half the job — being an attentive listener is equally powerful. I learned how to enter a discussion respectfully, contribute relevant and unique points, and build on others’ ideas instead of merely competing. The session also showed how teamwork, respect, and staying focused on the topic can make a discussion productive rather than chaotic. I became aware of habits like interrupting, straying from the topic, or using an overly aggressive tone — and how to consciously avoid them. This experience shaped my perspective on communication in group settings, helping me see discussions as opportunities for collective growth rather than individual victories.

Week-8 : Interview Skills

Today's Learning

This week’s focus on interview skills gave me a much-needed lens into how to represent myself professionally with clarity, authenticity, and calmness. I learned how to prepare well-thought-out responses for typical questions like "Tell me about yourself", "What are your strengths and weaknesses?" or "Describe a challenge you faced". Using the STAR technique (Situation, Task, Action, Result), I practiced turning my real-life experiences into powerful answers. I also worked on body language — sitting upright, smiling naturally, and maintaining eye contact — to convey confidence even before speaking. We had mock interviews that revealed my nervous tics and areas of improvement, but more importantly, they gave me the courage to be myself. I now understand that interviews are not just evaluations — they’re opportunities to show who I truly am and what I bring to the table.

Do's & Don'ts

Do's: Research the company, dress professionally, greet confidently, and give structured answers.
Don'ts: Don’t lie, interrupt, show arrogance, or criticize past employers.
